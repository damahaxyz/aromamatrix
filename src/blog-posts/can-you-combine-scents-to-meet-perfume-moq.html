Several scents cannot automatically be combined to meet one perfume MOQ. Each fragrance creates its own liquid batch, identification, filling changeover and artwork controls.

What can sometimes be shared

Plain bottles, pumps, caps and a common carton structure may be purchased across variants if the specifications are identical. Labels, printed scent names and fragrance liquid usually remain separate minimums.

Ask four questions

  • Is MOQ per fragrance, artwork, bottle design or total order?
  • Which component has the highest supplier minimum?
  • Will surplus packaging be stored or delivered?
  • What changeover and inspection work is added?

A smaller multi-scent launch is more practical with one bottle system and simple scent identification. The factory must review the actual split; combining quantities is an option, not a standard entitlement.
