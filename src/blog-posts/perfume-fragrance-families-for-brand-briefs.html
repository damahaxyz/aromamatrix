Perfume fragrance families give a brand and perfumer a shared starting vocabulary, but they should not become rigid boxes.

Citrus and aromatic directions can communicate brightness; floral structures range from transparent to opulent; woody styles may feel dry, creamy or smoky; amber directions often emphasize warmth and resins; gourmand ideas use edible associations; chypre and fougère describe recognizable structural traditions.

Use families with contrasts

“Woody” becomes useful when paired with choices such as clean versus smoky, dry versus sweet, intimate versus projecting. Add the customer, occasion, disliked notes and desired dry-down. References should explain an aspect, not demand an exact duplicate.

The brief should leave enough space for creative interpretation while making it possible to explain why one sample fits better than another.
