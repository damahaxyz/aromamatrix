The AROMAMATRIX project team coordinates international inquiries, quotations and sales communication. 4. Free Fragrance Sample Policy

Basic fragrance evaluation samples are free for suitable B2B projects. A typical evaluation set contains 3 × 10ml fragrance samples and normally takes 5–7 business days to prepare after the brief and sample direction are confirmed. The customer pays the actual courier cost based on destination, and the courier quotation is confirmed before dispatch.

Special formulations, extra revision rounds, custom packaging samples, urgent requests or unusual shipping requirements may require separate review before work continues.

5. Production Projects and Customer-Supplied Materials

Confirmed perfume production is carried out by Guangzhou Zhuoya Biotechnology Cosmetics Co., Ltd. or another manufacturing arrangement expressly identified in the project documents.

Mass production should proceed only after the relevant fragrance, formula direction, packaging components, artwork, specification and approval samples are confirmed. If you provide formulas, bottles, packaging, artwork, trademarks, reference samples or technical requirements, you are responsible for ensuring that you have the right to use and supply them.

Customer-supplied components may require incoming inspection, compatibility review, filling trials and schedule confirmation. Delays, shortages or defects in supplied materials may affect production timing, cost or feasibility.

AROMAMATRIX can provide available manufacturing and product information for buyer review, but the brand owner, importer, distributor or appointed responsible party remains responsible for final label approval, market registration, product claims, safety substantiation and destination-market legal compliance unless otherwise agreed in writing.

Factory certificates, SDS, COA, IFRA-related documents or batch documents do not automatically mean that a finished product is approved for sale in every country. The required documents depend on the formula, product type, destination market and agreed project scope.

7. Shipping and Dangerous Goods

Alcohol-based perfume is commonly handled as dangerous goods for international transport. Shipping method, documents, packing, route and cost are confirmed according to formula, alcohol content, capacity, quantity and destination. Delivery estimates are not guaranteed unless expressly confirmed by the carrier and commercial agreement.

Customers should inspect goods promptly after arrival and keep cartons, labels, photos and videos if damage, leakage or inconsistency is found. Any remedy will be reviewed based on evidence, the approved specification and the written commercial terms.
